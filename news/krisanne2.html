Some say that we're in a post-constitutional era, others believe that calls to return to a government restrict by the Constitution is an extremist ideology, still more opine that our Constitution is a "living document" one that needs to "change with the times".

However our Constitution still stands today as the "Law of the Land" which represents an idea that our government should be formed not to look after what's best for the collective or a particular class of people, but that it should protect the rights of the individual. The idea of inalienable individual rights, rights not to be abridged. A new concept, for a new country, at the time of its founding. 

We live in a time though, when there seems to be an increasing attempt to divide us into groups, defined by the different groups particular, or special interests. Interests that pitt one group against another with competing interests, while assigning new and never before recognized, special "group rights".

Come out and learn why the original idea of Individual Liberty should still be important to us today.
